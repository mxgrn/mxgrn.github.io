I'm Max Gorin

I've been in love with web-development since 2007, mainly credit to Ruby on Rails. Recently, I'm most excited to work with Elixir and Elm. I love producing polished UIs, as well as clean and well-architectured code.

Open source work

During a few years, I developed and maintained Netzke - an ExtJS/Rails library, that helps building desktop-like web-application in a conveniently modular way. I gave a few talks on Netzke at Ruby conferences around the world, one example being RubyKaigi 2013 in Tokyo.

Freelancing

I've worked with a number of companies from Europe, Asia and the US. As I'm very mobile, I prefer spending the initial month working on-site, so that I get closely acquainted with the project and the team, and then I switch to working remotely for another few months. I choose to work 6 hours a day, 4 days a week, so that I'm always focused, fresh and productive.

Here's an example of feedback I occasionally get from developers I work with:

I’m very happy with having you on the team. I like how you are just part of the team, like the rest, despite being remote. I especially like how I don’t have to worry about whether you are stuck or anything; I know if something comes up, you’ll let us know.

--- Arjan van der Gaag, received on Slack on April 25, 2016.
